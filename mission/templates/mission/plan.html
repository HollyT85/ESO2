Our Mission

Founded as a registered UK charity (CIO) in April 2025, we exist to make Earth Science accessible to everyone.

We advance education in Geology, Meteorology, Oceanography, Physical Geography, and Environmental Science by opening doors to those who have traditionally been excluded from these fields — including young people, individuals without formal qualifications, and communities facing disadvantage. We connect communities with leading scientific and cultural institutions, creating pathways into lifelong learning, vocational opportunities, and meaningful engagement with the natural world.

Through public events, fieldwork, workshops, digital resources, and partnerships across the UK, we use Earth Science as a foundation for understanding our planet, responding to climate change, and building a more sustainable and equitable future.

Earth Science Outreach UK is built upon a team with over a decade of experience running the Yorkshire Fossil Festival (YFF), which has taken place in Whitby, Redcar, Hull, and Scarborough since 2014. Our work includes field trips to explore the fossil sites along Yorkshire’s stunning Jurassic coastline.

Previously operating under the name GeoHeritage Rocks, we are now evolving into Earth Science Outreach UK (CIO), expanding our focus beyond Geology to encompass all Earth Science disciplines, including Climate Change.

In addition to the Yorkshire Fossil Festival, we have provided engaging workshops for children at the Lyme Regis Fossil Festival and set up the “Rock Showman’s Booth”—an interactive venue where visitors can learn about Earth’s history through displays and short presentations on minerals, fossils, and other Earth Science topics. You can find videos of past talks and interviews on our website.

Looking ahead, we plan to apply for funding to expand our outreach efforts, offering year-round visits to schools and encouraging people to explore their local Earth History. F or more information, see Our Mission.

In 20204, the Yorkshire Fossil Festival (held from May 25-27, 2024 in Redcar) welcomed over 17,000 visitors and featured interactive workshops, guest speakers, and information stands. The event highlighted leading organisations in Earth Science, such as the British Geological Survey. Check out Our Partners for more details.

We are also regular participants at the Lyme Regis Fossil Festival, the largest event of its kind in the UK. At these festivals, we engage in Earth Science edutainment, with hands-on displays and performances through The Rock Showman’s Booth.

Through these events, we have built an extensive network of people and institutions involved in Earth Science outreach across the UK, and we continue to be active in the global Earth Science community. Our goal is to formalise this network into a Registered Charity, bringing Earth Science to communities that lack access to these vital educational resources. See Our Mission for more details.
